Chingling are an important part of many sacred rituals. This gives them a special status in religious circles, and traditionalist towns may even ban battling Chingling. To hear a Chingling walking in the forest is an omen of good luck: follow the sound, and you should find fortune.

Chimecho tend to attach themselves to any flat underside, and chatter endlessly if the object they’re stuck to moves. They’re popular on fairgrounds, drawing in children with their friendly chimes. Construction sites find them less charming.
